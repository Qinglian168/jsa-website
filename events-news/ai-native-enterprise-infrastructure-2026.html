1Cisco & NVIDIA: Secure AI Factory Goes Pod-Scale

On March 16, 2026, Cisco announced a major expansion of its Secure AI Factory with NVIDIA, giving enterprises a unified framework for deploying AI across their entire infrastructure — from central data centers to edge sites where real-time decisions cannot wait. As Cisco Chair and CEO Chuck Robbins stated: “In partnership with NVIDIA, we're solving that challenge with an architecture that sets a new standard for performance — making it simpler to deploy, operate, and secure AI infrastructure.”

Key Announcements from Cisco & NVIDIA:

  • Cisco N9100 with NVIDIA Spectrum-6 Ethernet — A new 102.4 Tbps high-speed switch powering the most demanding AI workloads, joining the generally available 800G N9100 with Spectrum-4
  • Cisco Nexus Hyperfabric (Nexus One) — Now supports N9000 Series switches including N9100 with NVIDIA Spectrum-X, transforming complex multi-vendor integration into a simple full-stack solution that cuts deployment timelines from months to weeks
  • Hybrid Mesh Firewall on NVIDIA BlueField DPUs — Policy enforcement extended to NVIDIA BlueField data processing units embedded in GPU servers, blocking threats at the server level before they reach enterprise data
  • Cisco AI Defense + NVIDIA NeMo Guardrails — Purpose-built guardrails for AI agents, securing agent-to-agent interactions at edge locations. Cisco also announced support for NVIDIA's OpenShell agent development platform
  • Edge AI with NVIDIA RTX PRO 4500 Blackwell — Enterprises can now run mission-critical AI inference at the edge across Cisco UCS and Unified Edge portfolios without data-center-scale energy costs

NVIDIA CEO Jensen Huang emphasized: “AI factories are transforming every industry, and security must be built into every layer — from silicon to software — to protect data, applications, and infrastructure. Together, NVIDIA and Cisco are building the secure foundation for AI infrastructure — core to edge.” This partnership gives enterprises two validated deployment paths: an NVIDIA Cloud Partner (NCP)-compliant reference architecture and a Cisco Silicon One-based architecture.

2NVIDIA Vera Rubin: Seven Chips, One AI Supercomputer

At GTC 2026, NVIDIA unveiled the Vera Rubin platform— a generational leap comprising seven new chips now in full production, designed to scale the world's largest AI factories. The platform brings together the NVIDIA Vera CPU, Rubin GPU, NVLink 6 Switch, ConnectX-9 SuperNIC, BlueField-4 DPU, Spectrum-6 Ethernet switch, and the newly integrated Groq 3 LPU.

Vera Rubin Platform Components:

Vera Rubin NVL72 Rack

  • • 72 Rubin GPUs + 36 Vera CPUs connected by NVLink 6
  • • Trains large MoE models with 1/4 the GPUs vs. Blackwell
  • • Up to 10x higher inference throughput per watt
  • • 1/10th the cost per token
  • • Scales with Quantum-X800 InfiniBand & Spectrum-X Ethernet

Groq 3 LPX Inference Rack

  • • 256 LPU processors with 128GB on-chip SRAM
  • • 640 TB/s scale-up bandwidth
  • • Up to 35x higher inference throughput per megawatt
  • • Optimized for trillion-parameter models
  • • Million-token context support

BlueField-4 STX Storage Rack

  • • AI-native shared storage infrastructure
  • • DOCA Memos KV cache processing
  • • Boosts inference throughput by up to 5x
  • • Pod-wide context for multi-turn agent interactions

Spectrum-6 SPX Ethernet Rack

  • • Accelerates east-west traffic across AI factories
  • • Spectrum-X Ethernet Photonics with co-packaged optics
  • • Up to 5x greater optical power efficiency
  • • 10x higher resiliency vs. pluggable transceivers

Jensen Huang called Vera Rubin “a generational leap — seven breakthrough chips, five racks, one giant supercomputer — built to power every phase of AI.” The platform also introduces DSX Max-Q software enabling 30% more AI infrastructure within a fixed-power data center, and DSX Flex unlocking 100 gigawatts of stranded grid power. Global system manufacturers including Cisco, Dell, HPE, and Lenovo are expected to deliver Vera Rubin-based servers starting H2 2026.

For enterprise buyers, the implications are profound: AI infrastructure cost-per-token is dropping by an order of magnitude, making large-scale agentic AI deployment economically viable for the first time. Enterprises investing in NVIDIA-accelerated Cisco UCS servers today are positioned to seamlessly adopt Vera Rubin racks as they become available.
